# US B-1/B-2 Visa — Detailed Reference

Sources:
- https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/tourism-visit/visitor.html
- https://www.usa.gov/tourist-visa

---

## Visa Types: Full Activity Breakdown

### B-1 (Business Visitor) — Allowed
- Consulting with business associates
- Attending scientific, educational, professional, or business conventions or conferences
- Settling an estate
- Negotiating contracts
- Short-term training (not replacing a local worker)

### B-2 (Tourism/Pleasure) — Allowed
- Tourism and vacations
- Visiting friends or relatives
- Medical treatment (must demonstrate ability to pay)
- Social events (parties, weddings, graduations)
- Amateur participation in sports events, music competitions (unpaid only)
- Recreational study or courses not for degree credit (e.g., a short language course)

### B-1/B-2 — Not Allowed on Any Visitor Visa
- Employment or paid work of any kind
- Paid performances (sports, music, acting)
- Enrollment in a full course of study (use F-1 instead)
- Working as a member of a foreign press, radio, film, or other information media (use I visa)
- Arriving as a crewmember of a ship or aircraft (use D visa)
- Establishing permanent residence or adjusting status (while intent is present)
- Birth tourism (entering primarily to give birth for citizenship purposes) — specifically prohibited

## Visa Waiver Program (VWP)

Citizens of 42 countries may travel to the US without a visa for up to 90 days per visit for tourism or business if they:
1. Obtain ESTA approval (https://esta.cbp.dhs.gov) — typically valid 2 years / multiple entries
2. Have a valid e-passport (machine-readable with electronic chip)
3. Do not intend to work or study

VWP countries include: Andorra, Australia, Austria, Belgium, Brunei, Chile, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Japan, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Monaco, Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Poland, Portugal, San Marino, Singapore, Slovakia, Slovenia, South Korea, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Taiwan, UK.

**VWP limitations:** No extensions possible; overstaying even one day has serious consequences; cannot change status to most other visa categories from inside the US.

## DS-160 Form Details

**URL:** https://ceac.state.gov/genniv/

**Key sections:**
- Personal information (name as in passport, DOB, gender, nationality)
- Passport information (number, issue/expiry dates, place of issue)
- US contact information (person you're visiting or hotel name/address)
- US travel history (all prior visits — be exact; CBP has records)
- Current/past employment
- Education history
- Family information (parents, spouse, children)
- Security and background questions (15+ yes/no questions)
- Social media usernames (required since 2019)

**Photo specifications:**
- 2×2 inches (51×51 mm)
- Color, white or off-white background
- Taken within the last 6 months
- Face must cover 50–69% of the frame, looking directly at camera
- No glasses (since 2016 policy)
- No head coverings (except for religious reasons, with a separate statement)

**After submission:** Cannot be edited. If you discover a significant error, you must start a new DS-160 (new application ID). Minor errors can sometimes be corrected at the interview.

## Fee Structure

| Fee | Amount | When | Refundable? |
|:----|:-------|:-----|:------------|
| MRV (Machine Readable Visa) fee | $185 USD | Before scheduling appointment | No |
| Issuance fee (reciprocity) | Varies by nationality ($0–$200+) | After approval, before passport return | No |
| ESTA (VWP alternative) | $21 USD | At time of ESTA application | No |

**MRV fee validity:** Typically valid for 1 year from payment. If you don't use it to schedule and attend an interview within that window, it expires.

**Reciprocity fees:** Based on what the applicant's home country charges US citizens for equivalent visas. Check by nationality: https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/Visa-Reciprocity-and-Civil-Documents-by-Country.html

## Appointment Scheduling Portals

Scheduling is done through third-party contractors, not directly through State Department. The correct portal depends on the country:

| Region/Country | Portal |
|:---------------|:-------|
| Most countries | https://www.ustraveldocs.com (CGI Federal) |
| Canada | https://www.vfsglobal.ca/usa/canada/ |
| UK | https://www.ustraveldocs.com/uk |
| India | https://www.ustraveldocs.com/in |
| Mexico | https://www.ustraveldocs.com/mx |

To create an account, you need:
- MRV fee payment confirmation/receipt number
- DS-160 confirmation barcode number
- Passport number

**Appointment types:** Some countries require a separate biometrics appointment at a Visa Application Center (VAC) before the consulate interview. Others take fingerprints at the consulate.

## Interview Waiver (Dropbox)

Some applicants may qualify to renew without an in-person interview if:
- Applying within 48 months of a previous US visa expiration in the same category
- Under age 14 or over age 79
- OR specific country programs at individual consulates

Eligibility varies by consulate. Check the embassy website for the target country.

## After the Interview: Possible Outcomes

### Approved (Visa Issued)
- Passport retained, returned by courier with visa stamped
- Typical turnaround: 2–5 business days after approval
- Verify all details on the visa: name, DOB, visa type, number of entries, validity dates

### 221(g) Administrative Processing
- Visa is not approved or denied yet — additional review required
- Officer gives a form specifying if additional documents are needed
- Can take days to several months
- Track: https://ceac.state.gov/ceac/
- Common reasons: security checks, additional document review, specialized clearances

### 214(b) Refusal (Most Common Denial)
- Applicant failed to demonstrate nonimmigrant intent (i.e., officer not convinced they'll return home)
- Legal basis: INA Section 214(b)
- Can reapply immediately with stronger evidence of ties to home country
- No required waiting period, but reapplying with the same weak evidence is unlikely to succeed

### Other Refusal Grounds
- 212(a) inadmissibility: prior overstay, criminal history, health grounds, prior removal
- Some grounds are waivable with Form I-192; consult an immigration attorney

## Important Rules and Warnings

**Overstaying:** Staying past the date on Form I-94 (not the visa expiration date — different thing) automatically voids the visa under INA §222(g) and triggers:
- 3-year bar for overstays of 180–364 days
- 10-year bar for overstays of 365+ days

**Valid visa in expired passport:** Still valid. Travel with both the old passport (with visa) and new passport.

**Visa ≠ Admission:** The visa allows you to seek entry at the border. CBP officers at ports of entry make the final admission decision and set the authorized period of stay (shown on Form I-94, now electronic at https://i94.cbp.dhs.gov).

**Changing status inside the US:** Possible through USCIS (e.g., from B-2 to F-1) if you had nonimmigrant intent at the time of entry and meet other requirements. Cannot change to some categories (e.g., H-1B cap-subject). Consult an attorney.

**Travel during pending change of status:** Generally inadvisable without attorney guidance — it may abandon the pending application.
